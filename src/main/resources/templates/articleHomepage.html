                    ─ We want evolution for the future not plastic pollution

Australian households nowadays rely heavily on plastics usage in daily life, and they are non-biodegradable plastics. Non-biodegradable plastics are not recyclable or landfilled since the components are not easily broken down and decompose. As a result, it has caused severe environmental pollution and social issues and even caused a huge negative impact on the Australian economy. Hence, Victoria state authorities and relevant local councils set a series of policies and environmental saving solutions to mitigate and address the issues.

What a waste: An analysis of household expenditure on food

tags: Household waste; Food waste

Australian households are throwing out more than $5 billion worth of food each year, more than Australians spend on digital equipment, and more than it costs to run the Australian Army. In addition to the direct financial costs of this waste, the environmental impact associated with excessive greenhouse gas emissions and water use is substantial. This paper examines who is wasting food and the motivations behind this behaviour.

https://australiainstitute.org.au/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/PB-6-What-a-waste-final_7.pdf
